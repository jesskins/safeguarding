What is Safeguarding?


Summary

Safeguarding refers to the proactive measures taken to protect individuals, particularly vulnerable groups such as children and adults at risk, from harm, abuse, neglect, and exploitation. It involves creating environments that promote well-being, health, and human rights while preventing potential risks.

Key Points:

Protection from Harm

Ensures that individuals are kept safe from physical, emotional, and sexual abuse, neglect, or exploitation.

Preventative Approach

Involves early identification of risks and implementing measures to reduce the likelihood of harm occurring.

Rights and Well-being

Safeguarding respects the rights of individuals, focusing on their dignity, autonomy, and overall well-being.

Multi-Agency Collaboration

Effective safeguarding often involves collaboration between various organizations (e.g., healthcare, education, social services) to provide comprehensive protection.

Legal and Ethical Framework

Safeguarding is underpinned by laws, policies, and guidelines to ensure consistent and effective practices across sectors.

These principles help ensure that individuals, particularly those who may be more vulnerable, are supported and kept safe in their everyday environments.
